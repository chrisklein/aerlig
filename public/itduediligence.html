Information technology is obviously a powerful tool for the management and operation of an enterprise. IT is also one of the largest capital investment and operational expense areas for a company in any industry. The challenge, for a business owner, CEO, or potential investor, is that technology changes rapidly and business strategies based on existing paradigms can quickly become outdated. The other challenge encountered by many businesses is that the business people and technology people come from different worlds, speak different languages, think in different ways, and frequently do not understand each other very well which can result in numerous problems.

 How Aerlig can help.

All of the senior professionals in Aerlig's IT Strategy and Due Diligence group are former leaders of companies whose core offering was a technology product or service. As a result, each of them is a rare individual with significant depth in both business and technology. Aerlig can help CEO's, shareholders, and potential investors assess a company's corporate strategy, IT strategy, IT execution, and IT staff to answer a range of questions including:

  • Are my company's business and technology strategies aligned?
  • Are my assumptions about future technology spending levels reasonable given my company's goals?
  • Are there additional competitive advantages that could be obtained through IT?
  • Could my company save money with an alternative IT strategy?
  • If I acquire another company, what are our prospects for successfully integrating our IT?
  • Should my company make changes to our IT organization or staff?
  • Which IT functions should we outsource?
  • Do we, or will we, face significant security, continuity, or downtime risks?
  • If we want to separate one of our business units (perhaps for its sale), how will that impact our technology costs and capabilities?
