 Why you might need help.
For most business owners, their company is their largest single investment, especially when "sweat equity" is taken into account. The conversion of that investment into cash and the transfer of control of the associated employees, assets, and external relationships to another party is undeniably a highly critical emotional and financial moment. The process is made more complex when sellers are asked to retain certain types of liabilities, to retain a portion of the equity, and to agree to non-compete provisions, all while trying to simultaneously optimize the tax implications of the sale. As a result, structuring the sale of a private company presents significant additional variables that can make or break the transaction's success from the seller's point of view.
