Finding success when acquiring a company is enormously challenging. There are three critical phases to any purchase. First, a prospective buyer must identify candidates for acquisition based on strategic, geographic, cultural, financial, and other criteria. Next, a buyer needs to navigate the transaction itself, from the initial approach to due diligence, valuation, structuring, and closing. Finally, a buyer needs to successfully integrate the acquisition in order to maximize potential cost savings and to reap the rewards of potential synergies. Aerlig's senior professionals can help with custom designed services tailored to each phase of the process.

 Phase 1: Identifying acquisition targets.
The task of identifying a set of attractive acquisition targets is both complex and time consuming. Too often, organizations only consider their direct competitors as potential merger or acquisition targets, or, even worse, only evaluate potential transactions that are brought to them and do not proactively seek out new M&A opportunities at all. In our view, the search for M&A targets should be a continuous process guided by and integrated with the organization's business and financial strategies. Aerlig can help, either on a relatively short term project basis or on an ongoing monthly engagement basis where we essentially serve as an outsourced M&A department for organizations that do not wish to incur the expense of dedicated M&A staff.
 Phase 2: Navigating a transaction.
There are few moments in the life of a business where more value can be created or destroyed in a short period of time than the process of buying another private company. First, a buyer must determine the true value of a potential acquisition, then negotiate purchase price and structure, then (if necessary) arrange financing, and finally make it through documentation and closing. The process involves a compressed, pressure packed workload that can detract from other business priorities, is fraught with huge opportunities and pitfalls, and can often become emotional on both sides of the negotiating table. Aerlig's senior professionals have completed hundreds of transactions, and can help off-load work, provide uncolored and actionable business-focused advice, and bring experienced perspective should unexpected challenges arise.
 Phase 3: Post-merger integration.
When acquisitions fail to meet expectations, the failure is often due to poor integration planning and execution. Integration planning should begin well before closing, and should include conscious decisions on the degree of operational integration, customer communications and marketing related to the merger, the post-merger organization structure, the assignments of key executives, and the methods and targets for the integration of cultures. If the integration strategy and the initial investment thesis are not congruent, then it is likely that the merger will end up being deemed a disappointment.
